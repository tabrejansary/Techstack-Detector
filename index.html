Usage Guide

Learn how to make the most of TechStack Detector

Installation

Visit the Chrome Web Store and click "Add to Chrome" to install the extension. It will automatically appear in your browser toolbar.

Basic Usage

Navigate to any website you want to analyze, then click the TechStack Detector icon in your toolbar. The popup will automatically scan and display detected technologies.

Understanding Results

Technologies are organized into 7 intuitive categories. Click category headers to expand or collapse sections. Each technology is displayed with a visual badge.

Exporting Reports

Click the "Copy Report" button to copy a formatted list of all detected technologies to your clipboard. Use this for documentation, analysis, or presentations.

Retrying Detection

If a website doesn't show any technologies initially, click the "Retry" button to run the detection again. Some sites may require additional time to load all resources.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about TechStack Detector

How does TechStack Detector work?

TechStack Detector uses multiple detection methods including analyzing DOM elements, JavaScript variables, HTTP headers, cookies, and meta tags to identify technologies used on a website.

Is my browsing data collected?

No, TechStack Detector processes all data locally in your browser. No information is sent to external servers, ensuring your privacy and security.

Why are some technologies not detected?

Some technologies may use obfuscation or may not leave detectable traces in the frontend. Backend technologies are detected via HTTP headers when available.

Can I use this for competitive analysis?

Yes, TechStack Detector is perfect for competitive analysis, market research, and understanding technology trends across different websites and industries.

Is the extension free to use?

Yes, TechStack Detector is completely free with no limitations. You can use it to analyze as many websites as you want.
